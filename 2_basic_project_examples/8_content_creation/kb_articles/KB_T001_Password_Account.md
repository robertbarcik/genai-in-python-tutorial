## User Account Locked After Failed Login Attempts – How to Resolve

If your account is locked after too many failed logins, you’re not alone. This can be frustrating and time-consuming, but it’s a protective measure to keep your information secure. Follow these simple steps to regain access smoothly.

Problem statement
- You attempted to sign in multiple times and your account became locked. An automated directory service delay may also require a short wait before the lock is released.

What happened (in plain terms)
- A security feature temporarily locks accounts after several unsuccessful login attempts.
- An Active Directory synchronization delay can affect the lock status. A forced or manual sync may be performed by the support team.
- You may need to wait a brief period, reset your password, and then sign in again after the reset completes.

Step-by-step recovery instructions
1) Wait for automatic unlock (usually about 15 minutes)
- After the lockout occurs, the system often unlocks itself automatically after a short period.
- Do not attempt to log in during this window, as repeated attempts can extend the lockout.

2) If the automatic unlock does not occur, use the Reset in the Portal
- Open your account portal (the same place you normally sign in).
- Choose the option to reset your account or password.
- Confirm your identity if prompted (you may be asked to answer security questions or receive a verification code).

3) Allow a short processing time after the reset
- After you submit the reset request, wait 2–3 minutes before trying to sign in again.
- This pause gives the system time to apply the reset and update your access.

4) Sign in with your refreshed credentials
- Return to the login page and enter your username and the new password or temporary credentials provided.
- If prompted, complete any additional verification steps (for example, a code sent to your email or phone).

5) If you are still locked or cannot sign in
- Contact support for a manual unlock or to confirm the status of directory synchronization.
- If advised, a forced synchronization of the directory service might be performed on your behalf.

Troubleshooting — common issues
- Issue: You tried to sign in immediately after a reset.
  Resolution: Wait 2–3 minutes after the reset, then attempt login again.
- Issue: You made multiple login attempts during the lockout.
  Resolution: Avoid repeated attempts during the lockout period; wait for auto-unlock or follow the reset steps.
- Issue: Directory synchronization delay persists.
  Resolution: Support can perform a forced synchronization to ensure your account status is refreshed.

Abbreviations and technical terms explained
- AD (Active Directory): A directory service that manages user accounts and permissions within an organization.
- Synchronization: The process of updating information across systems so that access status is current.

Warnings — common mistakes to avoid
- Do not attempt to log in during the lockout period, as this can extend the lockout.
- Do not submit multiple reset requests in quick succession; follow the 2–3 minute guideline after a reset.
- Do not ignore verification prompts; complete them to ensure your identity is confirmed.

Still need help?
If you continue to experience trouble or the lockout persists beyond the recommended wait times, please reach out to our support team. Provide your username and any error messages you see to help us assist you more quickly. We’re here to help you get back to work as soon as possible.
