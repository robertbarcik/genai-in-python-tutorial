## Outlook on Mobile Is Not Syncing Emails: Quick Fix

If your mobile device isn’t syncing emails, you may miss important messages and feel frustrated. This article walks you through a simple, reliable fix to get Outlook syncing again.

Problem statement
Mobile Outlook is not updating your inbox and new messages aren’t appearing in real time, even though you can access your mailbox from a computer.

Step-by-step solution
1) Remove the email account from Outlook on your mobile device
- Open the Outlook app.
- Go to Settings (gear icon) > your email account > Remove account.
- Confirm the removal when prompted. This cleans out old or corrupted settings.

2) Clear the Outlook cache
- Close the Outlook app completely.
- Depending on your device, clear the app cache:
  - iPhone/iPad: There is no manual cache clear; reinstalling the app serves the same purpose.
  - Android: Settings > Apps > Outlook > Storage > Clear Cache.
- Reopen Outlook after clearing the cache.

3) Re-add the email account
- In the Outlook app, choose Add Account.
- Enter your email address and password when prompted.
- If you see a prompt about modern authentication, select Yes or Enable to continue.
- Complete any two-factor authentication steps if required by your organization or email provider.

4) Enable modern authentication when prompted
- Modern authentication uses more secure login methods (for example, if your organization requires it). If your device asks to enable modern authentication, approve it.
- If you are unsure, contact your IT department or email provider to confirm whether modern authentication is required for your account.

5) Verify that you are syncing
- Allow a few minutes for the initial synchronization to complete.
- Open your inbox and confirm that new emails appear and folders update correctly.

Troubleshooting common issues
- Emails still not syncing after re-adding:
  - Ensure you are connected to the internet (Wi-Fi or cellular data).
  - Check that you entered the correct password and, if applicable, used the latest password.
  - Verify that the account is configured with the correct server settings if your organization uses custom configurations.
- Old password error:
  - If you changed your password recently, sign out and sign back in with the new password.
  - Ensure you completed any required password updates in your identity provider.
- Modern authentication prompt not appearing:
  - Some devices or accounts may not require it. If the prompt doesn’t appear, continue with the steps and rely on the initial login flow.
  - Contact your IT administrator if you are unsure about the required authentication method.

Warnings: common mistakes to avoid
- Not clearing the cache before re-adding the account. This can leave stale data that prevents a fresh connection.
- Using an old or incorrect password during re-addition, which stops the sign-in process.
- Skipping the modern authentication step if your organization requires it; this can prevent full syncing.
- Re-adding the account too quickly without giving the system time to finish the initial setup.
